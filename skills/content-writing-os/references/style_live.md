# Active Style Guide

Style-Version: 1.0.0
Last-Updated: 2026-02-22
Source: style_baseline.md

## Primary Goal

Explain technical AI concepts deeply enough for practitioners while remaining clear for business owners who need decision-grade understanding.

## Voice Rules

1. Technical but clear.
2. Business-relevant, not purely academic.
3. Specific and measurable where possible.
4. Calm, practical, and direct.
5. No inflated claims.

## Required Framing

For every major section:
1. What it is
2. How it works
3. Why it matters to the business
4. When to use it and when not to use it

## Concept Coverage Priorities

1. AI fundamentals
2. LLM fundamentals
3. Agentic AI fundamentals
4. Architecture and tradeoffs
5. Value realization and operational impact

## Deep-Dive Clarity Rules

1. Define each major concept before diving into mechanics.
2. Show mechanism with practical examples, not abstract theory alone.
3. Translate each mechanism into business meaning:
- what changes in operations
- what changes in economics
- what changes in risk exposure
4. Close each major section with a decision implication for owners and operators.

## Value Translation Rules

Always connect technical explanation to at least one:
1. revenue impact
2. cost reduction
3. cycle-time reduction
4. quality or consistency gain
5. risk mitigation
